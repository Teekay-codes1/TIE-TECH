What is a Calorie Deficit?

A calorie deficit happens when you eat fewer calories than your body burns in a day. Your body then turns to stored fat for energy — which is how you lose weight.

For example, if your body burns 2,500 calories per day and you eat 2,000, you have a 500-calorie deficit. Over 7 days that's 3,500 calories — roughly 0.5 kg of fat lost.

What is BMR?

BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate) is the number of calories your body needs just to stay alive — breathing, heartbeat, organ function — if you did absolutely nothing all day.

What is TDEE?

TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure) is your BMR multiplied by your activity level. This is your true maintenance calorie number — eat this and your weight stays the same.

How much deficit is safe?

A deficit of 250–500 calories per day is generally considered safe and sustainable. Deficits above 1,000 calories per day can lead to muscle loss, fatigue, and nutritional deficiencies.

How is this calculated?

This calculator uses the Mifflin-St Jeor equation — the most accurate BMR formula available for most people, recommended by nutrition scientists worldwide.

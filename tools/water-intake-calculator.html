How Much Water Should You Drink?

Most people have heard "drink 8 glasses a day" — but that's a one-size-fits-all guess. Your actual water needs depend on your body weight, how active you are, and the temperature where you live.

This calculator uses your personal details to give you a more accurate daily target.

Why does weight matter?

Larger bodies have more cells, more blood, and more metabolic activity — all of which require water to function. A general baseline is around 35ml of water per kilogram of body weight per day.

Why does activity level matter?

When you exercise, you sweat and lose water rapidly. Active people can lose an extra 500ml–1 litre per hour of exercise, which must be replaced to stay properly hydrated.

Why does climate matter?

Hot weather causes you to sweat more even when resting. People living in tropical or hot climates need significantly more water than those in cold regions just to stay at the same hydration level.

Signs you're not drinking enough

Dark yellow urine, headaches, fatigue, dry mouth, and difficulty concentrating are all early signs of dehydration. Aim for pale yellow urine as a simple daily check.
